A Look into Korea

Hover over pictures for brief description
Seoul

Officially known as the Seoul Special City, it is the capital and largest metropolis of South Korea. Located along the Han River, it is the home of many UNESCO World Heritage Sites and many other things.

Jeju-Do

The island lies in the Korea Strait, south of the Korean Peninsula, it is South Korea's largest island. There are many things to see on the island, like visiting Dongmun Market or going to visit ManJangGul Cave, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Busan

Officially known as Busan Metropolitan City, it is South Korea's second most populous city after Seoul. It is located on the southeastern tip of the Korean Peninsula. One of the main attractions of Busan is HaeUnDae Beach.

HanGang

The river flows through Seoul into the Yellow Sea. There is a HanGang Park in Seoul that many people go to on nice days to have picnics. Many people ride their bike along the river or do water activites such as wakeboarding or paddle boarding.

NamSan Tower

A communication and observation tower located on Nam Mountain in central Seoul. It is the second highest point in Seoul and is considered a local landmark. The most popular attractions are the Love Padlocks and the Namsan Cable Car.

SeoRakSan

The third highest mountain in South Korea, sometimes considered the backbone of South Korea. The Dinosaur Ridge on SeoRakSan is one of Korea's cultural scenic treasures and earned its name because mountain peaks in the ridge look like the back of a spiked dinosaur.

ChunCheon

Capital of GangWon Province and located in a basin formed by the Soyang River and Han River. Many festivals take place all throughout the year in ChunCheon that many people visit.

DMZ

A strip of land running across the Korean Peninsula near the 38th parallel north. The demilitarized zone is a border barrier that divides the peninsula roughly in half. It is conisdered a tourist attraction for many and is also a big part of Korea's history.

Gyeongju-si

A coastal city in the far southeastern corner of South Korea. Gyeongju-si is known as "the museum without walls" because of the vast number of cultural properties and archaeological sites of the city.
